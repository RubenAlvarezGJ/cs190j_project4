# Player Character
## idle

16-bit SNES-era pixel art, top-down overhead 3/4 perspective in the visual
lineage of A Link to the Past. Hand-pixeled look, clean dark outlines,
limited palette, no anti-aliasing, nearest-neighbor crisp edges.

SUBJECT: a lone post-apocalyptic survivor — the player character of a
zombie survival game. Pre-shrine "scavenger" look: rugged improvised
clothing (worn jacket, wrapped/taped boots, fingerless gloves, a cloth or
scarf), no armor yet. Lean, capable but vulnerable. Holding a simple
pickaxe in one hand. Character should read clearly from above.

CHARACTER SHEET: the SAME character shown in 4 separate idle poses —
facing DOWN (toward camera), facing UP (back to camera), facing RIGHT,
and facing LEFT — arranged in a single row on a transparent background.
Consistent proportions and palette across all four. Roughly 24x24 px per
character cell, scaled up cleanly for visibility.

PALETTE + MOOD: desaturated and muted — rust browns, ash grays, faded
military green, weathered cloth, dead-grass yellow. Gritty and worn, but
the figure should still be slightly warmer/lighter than a typical ruined
background so it stays readable. Tense survival tone, NOT gory or horror —
T-for-Teen, stylized.

FORMAT: pixel-art sprite reference sheet, transparent background,
limited palette, sharp pixels, no blur, no smoothing, no gradients.

CONSISTENCY: this is the master reference for the entire "fLoW" art set;
all later sprites (zombies, armored player, props) must match this exact
style, perspective, and palette.

## walking animations

16-bit SNES-era pixel art, top-down overhead 3/4 perspective in the visual style
of The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past. A four-direction WALK-CYCLE sprite
sheet for a post-apocalyptic scavenger boy.

Character (must match the attached idle exactly): messy, spiky dark-brown hair;
large expressive eyes visible above a faded military-green scarf pulled over the
lower half of his face; a worn rust-brown leather jacket with a popped collar and
a diagonal crossbody backpack strap; a small pouch at the hip; ragged charcoal
trousers; scuffed boots with cloth wrapped at the ankles. He carries a pickaxe
with a wooden haft and a gray steel head held in one hand. Unarmored, early-game
scavenger look.

Animation: a looping walk in four facings — down (toward camera), up (away), left,
and right. In each, the legs alternate through a natural stride with a subtle
vertical body bob; the free arm swings while the pickaxe stays held at his side /
resting on his shoulder.

Layout: one clean sprite sheet on a fully transparent background. A uniform grid,
one row per direction in the order down, up, left, right, with 4 evenly spaced,
identically sized frames per row, each character centered in its cell. Crisp
hard-edged pixels, nearest-neighbor scaling — no anti-aliasing, no blur, no
gradients or soft shading — and a small limited palette.

Palette & mood: desaturated and muted — rust browns, ash grays, weathered
concrete, dead-grass yellow — with the faded military-green scarf as the single
cool accent. Gritty, decayed, abandoned-world tone; threatening but stylized and
rated T-for-Teen, never gory. Sprite roughly 24 px tall in a 32×32 px cell,
rendered larger and downscaled cleanly. Match the attached idle sprite's
proportions, outfit, colors, and pickaxe.
